DETAILED DESCRTIPTION

1.Overview

Topic
The concept of our project is collected from the idea of assignment 1 of Tien and Chau, two of our members. In the first assignment, Tien offered an app that would provide home training routines and healthy meals to people who cannot afford gym memberships, allowing them to lose weight at home rather than visiting public locations, which could be dangerous in the event of a COVID-19 pandemic. Meanwhile, Chau has developed a platform called 'Smart Health Prediction,' which allows patients to input their health issues so that AI can forecast their existing disease status and provide relevant advice.

However, because the field of health care encompasses a wide range of diseases, and we lack knowledge in that area. Therefore, we decided to focus our efforts on obesity. Our app will have two unique features: the 3D image feature will scan the user's body and predict which muscle groups need to be improved, and the Artificial Intelligence feature will automatically calculate the Body Mass Index (BMI) - a weight-to-height ratio that can be used to determine whether a person is overweight or underweight - based on the users' current weight and height input.


5.Scope and Limits

A scope of work (SOW) of a project is important as it defines what will be included as a part of our project and what is not, and also the boundaries of the project (Toucan 2020). Therefore, the Business IT team will provide a specific scope statement about the purpose of this project, what we want to create and how to accomplish it.

Overall, this project is to create a mobile application - which can be functioned on both iOS and Android mobile devices - to raise awareness about doing workout and obesity. For better illustration of how our mobile application will help Vietnamese people, we have built our desired product's prototype on Figma. To know more about login function, the app's settings, etc please visit this link:

https://www.figma.com/file/LcIeUzKweaa0G2KuOWBK8R/Business-IT-team-(Our-FitnessPal)?node-id=6%3A0

The project will produces four main different features that strongly motivates Vietnamese to move and not being inactive: body scanning, meal planning, BMI calculation and automatical exercises suggested. In particular, one more feature is considered to be developed in the future - building a fitness community on the application.

1. Body Scanning (3D technology): users will turn on their front camera of smartphones to scan their body shapes, with some simple movement - so that the technology can scan different angles and produce digital body models that represent the user's body shapes. Moreover, users can zoom in, zoom out, rotate to identify which part of their body contains the most fat. There will also be a historical data section - where users can keep track of their progress by comparing their past, current and future body shapes.
2. BMI Calculator (AI Technology): our app will collect the Body Mass Index (BMI) of users to assess the probability of overweight through two ways:

  • Users will manually input their weight/ height and the system will automatically calculate the BMI for further comparison.
  • Secondly, users can use the BMI via facial recognition - this function will also utilise the 3D technology to scan the facial image that users have put in - to identify the BMI through the user's
    3. Propose suitable exercise plan (AI technology): based on the BMI and scanned image of the user's body, some suggested exercise will be listed out for users to choose. This is because each type of body will have a particular type of exercise. For instance, there is a men user that weighed 80kg - which is overweight, he can not practice the High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) because this type of exercise will require him to do a lot of jumping to burn fat. However, the overweight body will create pressure on his knees that could cause injuries, therefore, a different exercise plan must be suggested and provided for him to achieve his goal of losing weight.

    4. Meal planning (AI technology): according to our research, in order to lose weight, a suitable exercise is not enough, and a reasonable meal planning is very important (NHS n.d.). The most important principle for a person who wants to lose weight is they must control their “calories in smaller than calories out” - it means we have to eat less calories and move more. Therefore, our app will enable users to input the amount of food they are going to eat, and the app will calculate the calories equivalent to that amount of food - so that users can easily track their calorie intake.

    5. Fitness communities (still-in-consideration function): based on psychological research about physical excercise, people tend to enjoy their activities when they are doing it with a group of friends, or a community that shares the same interests, rather than taking exercise alone (Edwards 2003). Therefore, we aim to create a function that is like a social platform, where users can share their milestones, achievements in terms of fitness and sport, or find a friend that motivates and encourages them to take physical activity.

    Besides, our target audience is people who are struggling with being overweight but do not know where to start, or students, adults who have low income that can not afford gym membership fees to download our application as a real coach for their exercise target.

    9.Risks

    Poor UX/ UI integration

    • Cause
      • UI: not enough research go into the wireframe development that leads to unfriendly-user interface, lack of aesthetic attraction and hard for users to navigate lack
      • UX: Wrong choice of the servers, data diagrams, APIs that leads to poor functionality
    • Solution
      • UI: keep the app’s design simple, interactive with easy-to-absorb and consistent content. This could be solved by doing thorough research about the target audience to understand which features, functions they need from the moment they start logging our app until they get the needed data to help manage weight.
      • UX: be careful when building the wireframe that correctly maps to each stage of the customer journey, test several times before final releasing

    Unreliable content

    • Cause
      • With many misleading articles on the Internet, each piece of content published on the app needs to be accurate and reliable for users to follow. For example, outdated diet plans that adversely affect health should be avoided
    • Solution
      • Careful research about human’s health in general
      • Follow WHO’s data as a standard information for credibility
      • Take reference from nutritionists, doctors, health advisors, personal trainers when suggesting meal recipes and exercise plans
      • UX: be careful when building the wireframe that correctly maps to each stage of the customer journey, test several times before final releasing

    Inaccurate body scanner

    • Cause
      • wrong measurements resulted from the user's position and whether they are placing the back camera correctly, as well as technical issues might occur.
    • Solution
      • Providing informative and easy guidance with demo video showing users how to self-scan at home and how to interpret BMI ratios appeared after scanning.
      • Implementing preventive application maintenance to help ensure data security
      • Use simple-to-understand functions to enhance personalized monitoring to increase ease of use and boost users’ experience

    Security violation

    • Cause
      • Implementing unauthorized data storage systems, databases, inadequate encryption algorithms that are easy to be hacked. As people’s health is a sensitive issue, poor choice of user data protection can lead to an exposure of data to third parties for illegal purposes.
      • Inadequate IT knowledge when choosing security technologies
    • Solution
      • Understand thoroughly about privacy guidelines from Vietnamese government and invest in the most innovative security technologies
      • Have a section about data policy and be transparent about how users’ data will be used
      • Give users control over data the app collects
