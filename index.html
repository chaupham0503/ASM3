PROJECT DESCRIPTION

DETAILED DESCRTIPTION

1.Overview

Topic
The concept of our project is collected from the idea of assignment 1 of Tien and Chau, two of our members. In the first assignment, Tien offered an app that would provide home training routines and healthy meals to people who cannot afford gym memberships, allowing them to lose weight at home rather than visiting public locations, which could be dangerous in the event of a COVID-19 pandemic. Meanwhile, Chau has developed a platform called 'Smart Health Prediction,' which allows patients to input their health issues so that AI can forecast their existing disease status and provide relevant advice.

However, because the field of health care encompasses a wide range of diseases, and we lack knowledge in that area. Therefore, we decided to focus our efforts on obesity. Our app will have two unique features: the 3D image feature will scan the user's body and predict which muscle groups need to be improved, and the Artificial Intelligence feature will automatically calculate the Body Mass Index (BMI) - a weight-to-height ratio that can be used to determine whether a person is overweight or underweight - based on the users' current weight and height input.


Motivation
Our team's dedication to this project originates from our concern for people's health. We are still young and have countless opportunities to exercise in order to improve our health, rather than lying in bed or sitting in one spot for long periods of time - which is just as bad as smoking (Better Health Channel n.d.). Being passive over an incredibly long time can lead to obesity, chronic health problems, and even cancers (Better Health Channel n.d.).


Landscape
In the healthcare mobile application industry, few applications focus on the obesity people. Instead, many applications will provide the user with interactions with doctors and explain the users' symptoms, such as Better Help and Teladoc. Regarding nutrition, the market has Generis: DNA & Nutrition application. Specifically, only MySugr – Diabetes Tracker Log is an application that will track the diabetes journey of clients. However, none of these apps can combine all of the features that MyFitnessPal provides.

With MyFitnessPal, our application has five different features. First, Body scanning using 3D technology: use a phone camera for both operation IOS and Android to identify which part has the most fat in the body. Second, a BMI calculator using AI technology: have two methods to input the information. First method, users input height and weight manually. Another method is using AI technology to calculate BMI by facial image. Third, Suggested exercise for users using AI Technology: after collecting body scanning and BMI measurements, users can create their workout program or use the suggested exercises appropriate for their body. Fourth, Meal planning using AI technology: this features help users track the amoun of calories intake for a day which is the most critical element to control weights. Besides the main features, our team also provides one more unique feature: Fitness communities. The place where users can share their achievements or milestones on the journey of losing weight, share their meal recipe with personalized ingredients with other users, or simply it is just a place for everyone to find new friends and workout mates. This feature emerges since we know that getting in shape, explicitly escaping obesity, is a challenging journey. Therefore, this fitness community purposely motivates users to achieve their goals.

2.Aims

Nowadays, with an year-by-year acceleration in the overweight Vietnamese population, this is defined as an alerting signal for all of us to have awareness of serious diseases derived from obesity (i.e. they could be high risk of stroke, heart disease and several cancers etc), which in turn negatively impact our quality-of life, self-image and even self-esteem (Branson n.d.). Since we - our team are generation Z - who honestly do not pay attention too much to our health and exercises, we have together created this project with the aim to create incentives for everyone to have an active engagement in mitigating the risk of obesity as well as creating a healthy lifestyle. Specifically, we believe that this app could be acted as an initial step to help people gain a basic guideline of preventing obesity through the provision of appropriate standardized exercises and nutritious meal plan.

Regarding the first goal of our project, we desire to build a user-friendly mobile app which is easy to use/ implement by users and still ensure to satisfy the customers’ requirements about this app. In fact, we consider our app as an “all-service” app due to our provision of the fully user journey ranging from calculating BMI, detecting obesity, offering exercises, offering meal plans to having a social fitness. Therefore, having a successful, user-friendly app is our first goal.

In terms of the second goal, we want to make sure the accuracy of our provided result towards the body scanning, BMI calculator and having or not-having obesity decision. Since our app directly relates to people’s health, we have to follow absolute (100%) accuracy. This is not only about our reputation but also about medical ethics.

On the other hand, we also have another goal which is building a fitness community for our app users. We believe that through establishing social interaction, users could enhance their fitness motivations as they could together share their concerned obesity-related topics or even their specific milestones of this fitness-journey.

    9.Risks

    Poor UX/ UI integration

    • Cause
      • UI: not enough research go into the wireframe development that leads to unfriendly-user interface, lack of aesthetic attraction and hard for users to navigate lack
      • UX: Wrong choice of the servers, data diagrams, APIs that leads to poor functionality
    • Solution
      • UI: keep the app’s design simple, interactive with easy-to-absorb and consistent content. This could be solved by doing thorough research about the target audience to understand which features, functions they need from the moment they start logging our app until they get the needed data to help manage weight.
      • UX: be careful when building the wireframe that correctly maps to each stage of the customer journey, test several times before final releasing

    Unreliable content

    • Cause
      • With many misleading articles on the Internet, each piece of content published on the app needs to be accurate and reliable for users to follow. For example, outdated diet plans that adversely affect health should be avoided
    • Solution
      • Careful research about human’s health in general
      • Follow WHO’s data as a standard information for credibility
      • Take reference from nutritionists, doctors, health advisors, personal trainers when suggesting meal recipes and exercise plans
      • UX: be careful when building the wireframe that correctly maps to each stage of the customer journey, test several times before final releasing

    Inaccurate body scanner

    • Cause
      • wrong measurements resulted from the user's position and whether they are placing the back camera correctly, as well as technical issues might occur.
    • Solution
      • Providing informative and easy guidance with demo video showing users how to self-scan at home and how to interpret BMI ratios appeared after scanning.
      • Implementing preventive application maintenance to help ensure data security
      • Use simple-to-understand functions to enhance personalized monitoring to increase ease of use and boost users’ experience

    Security violation

    • Cause
      • Implementing unauthorized data storage systems, databases, inadequate encryption algorithms that are easy to be hacked. As people’s health is a sensitive issue, poor choice of user data protection can lead to an exposure of data to third parties for illegal purposes.
      • Inadequate IT knowledge when choosing security technologies
    • Solution
      • Understand thoroughly about privacy guidelines from Vietnamese government and invest in the most innovative security technologies
      • Have a section about data policy and be transparent about how users’ data will be used
      • Give users control over data the app collects

    Inadequate knowledge & skills

    • Cause
      • Wrong measurements resulted from the user's position and whether they are placing the back camera correctly, as well as technical issues might occur
    • Solution
      • Providing informative and easy guidance with demo video showing users how to self-scan at home and how to interpret BMI ratios appeared after scanning
      • Implementing preventive application maintenance to help ensure data security
      • Use simple-to-understand functions to enhance personalized monitoring to increase ease of use and boost users’ experience
